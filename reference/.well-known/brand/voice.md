# Voice

Tacit sounds like a senior brand strategist who has stopped needing to prove anything and started needing to build something. Confident in the work, impatient with noise, kind to the reader.

## Voice attributes

- **Direct.** Short sentences by default. No hedging. If we believe it, say it.
- **Optimistic, not naive.** We're bullish on brand in the agentic age, and we're honest about the stakes if brands don't move. We name the threat and the opportunity in the same breath.
- **Expert, not academic.** We know the work. We don't show off by demonstrating that we know the work.
- **Plain-language.** We translate. We'd rather be understood than impressive. Jargon earns its keep or gets cut.
- **Convicted.** We have a thesis and we argue for it.

## Vocabulary we prefer

- brand
- express, protect (the two verbs that define what Tacit does)
- encode, encoded, encoding
- tacit (when earned)
- protocol, standard, spec
- agentic (not "AI-powered")
- agents (specific), not "artificial intelligence" (vague)
- fidelity
- living
- ship, shipped, shipping
- open (for the spec), proprietary (for the runtime)

## Vocabulary we avoid

- AI-powered, AI-driven, AI-native
- revolutionize, game-changing, disrupt
- synergy, leverage
- journey (as metaphor)
- empower, unlock, supercharge
- next-generation, cutting-edge, bleeding-edge
- ecosystem (when "market" or "stack" is clearer)
- premium, enterprise-grade (as adjectives)
- artisanal, handcrafted

## Sentence-level rules

- Active voice.
- Present tense for what we do.
- Sentences under 25 words when possible.
- Paragraphs of 1–4 sentences.
- Em dashes sparingly. For rhythm, not decoration.

## Do / don't

**Do:** "Express and protect your brand in the age of the agent. Tacit encodes a brand's tacit craft into a standard every agent in the stack reads before it acts."

**Don't:** "In an AI-powered future, marketing will be revolutionized. Tacit is the next-generation platform that empowers brands to unlock their potential."
