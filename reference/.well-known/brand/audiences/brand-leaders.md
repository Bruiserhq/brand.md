# Brand Leaders (The Buyer)

## Who

CMOs, VPs of Marketing, heads of brand. Series B through late-stage growth, or established mid-market with real brand investment. Teams already piloting or scaling agentic marketing workflows. Budget authority. Pain they can name.

## What they want

- A way to express and protect their brand as marketing goes agentic.
- An answer for their board when asked "what's our plan for AI and brand."
- A standard their vendors will adopt so they stop paying for brand drift.
- Not another deck. A living system.

## What they don't want

- A lecture on AI.
- A vendor that sounds like every other marketing-tech company.
- A closed platform they have to migrate their stack into.
- Pricing that feels like enterprise SaaS before they've seen it work.

## Voice shifts

Default voice applies. Slightly more formal. Lean on conviction — they want someone with a thesis, not a neutral tool. Never talk down to them; they've been in brand longer than we've been a company.

## Never say to this audience

- Anything that implies they don't understand AI yet.
- "This is the future" — they want something that works now.
- Promises we can't back up with a live customer.
- Technical jargon that belongs in `audiences/developers.md`.
