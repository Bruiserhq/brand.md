# Platform Partners (The Distribution)

## Who

Product and partnerships leaders at platforms whose agents make decisions on the brand's behalf: Zefr, Canva, Webflow, Shopify, Adobe, Meta, The Trade Desk, Frontify, Typeface, DSPs, emerging creative-gen tools.

## What they want

- Faster, cleaner client onboarding.
- Better brand inputs into their agent stack.
- A differentiated story for their own market ("first platform to adopt the open brand standard").
- Low integration cost. Open spec they can consume, not another vendor lock-in.

## What they don't want

- To be told how to run their product.
- A spec that forces them to rebuild core infrastructure.
- To feel like Tacit is positioning itself between them and their customer.
- Lock-in or exclusivity that reduces their optionality.

## Voice shifts

More technical. Less emotional. Partnership language, not client language. "We integrate" not "we help." Lead with their pain before leading with our protocol. Respect their product roadmap — we slot in, we don't take over.

## Never say to this audience

- Anything positioning us as a competitor to their core product.
- Implying their existing brand-safety / brand-input layer is broken.
- Promising revenue share or business terms that aren't confirmed.
- "We'll send brands your way" without being able to back it up.
