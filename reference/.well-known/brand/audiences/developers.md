# Developers and Protocol Community

## Who

Engineers, technical PMs, protocol designers, AGENTS.md contributors, MCP community, open-source marketing-infrastructure builders. The people who decide whether a new standard is serious.

## What they want

- A well-specified protocol, not a marketing site.
- Code and examples. Reference implementations. A GitHub repo they can fork.
- Honest acknowledgment of prior art (AGENTS.md, MCP, schema.org, JSON-LD, robots.txt).
- Evidence we'll maintain this, not abandon it when VC funding dries up.

## What they don't want

- A company pretending its proprietary SaaS is an open standard.
- Spec-washing — vague hand-waving dressed up as a protocol.
- Closed governance. A cathedral, when they came for a bazaar.
- Marketing language in technical documentation.

## Voice shifts

Register tightens hard. Precise, technical, developer-native. No marketing adjectives. Lead with the spec. Acknowledge trade-offs explicitly. Cite prior art generously. Be the kind of company that earns trust by showing the work.

## Never say to this audience

- "AI-powered" anything.
- Claims the company sales team would make.
- "Enterprise-ready" without describing what that actually means.
- Vague versioning or lack of a changelog.
